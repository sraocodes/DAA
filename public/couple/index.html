Introduction

The PhenoRob Digital Agricultural Avatar (DAA) initiative is poised to redefine the boundaries of agronomic understanding. At this foundational stage, the DAA project envisions harnessing the power of coupling to bridge the complexities of crop modeling. From the nuanced molecular interactions within plant cells to vast regional climate patterns, the plan is to use coupling as an essential strategy in the DAA framework. By aiming to integrate various models in the future, the DAA aspires to offer a more comprehensive and accurate digital twin of the crop ecosystem.

Model Coupling

A key future endeavor of the PhenoRob DAA is to adopt model coupling. While specific implementations are still on the horizon, the goal is to amalgamate different models, ensuring the digital twin effectively represents the vast agronomic system. Through planned coupling techniques, the DAA aims to achieve several key benefits:

  1. 🌾 Augmented Accuracy: By leveraging the potential strengths of individual models for enhanced precision.
  2. 🌍 Holistic Representation: Expanding the scope to provide a comprehensive view as envisioned by the DAA project.
  3. 🌦️ Mitigated Shortcomings: Aiming to offset model limitations for a robust digital representation.
  4. 💧 Dynamic Feedbacks: Facilitating potential real-time interactions for enriched simulations.

Model Coupling Methods

The PhenoRob DAA has laid out an ambitious roadmap for incorporating various coupling methods to ensure seamless integration of models. While these techniques are part of the project’s future aspirations, they are foundational to the DAA’s goal of creating an accurate digital twin.

  1. 🌾 Sequential or One-way Data Transfer Coupling: Envisioned for simpler interactions within the DAA framework.
  2. 🌍 Loose Coupling: A planned approach for dynamic data exchange in the DAA’s multi-model setup.
  3. 🌦️ Shared Coupling: Aiming for models to share major components for deeper integration within DAA.
  4. 💧 Joined Coupling: An aspiration for deeper inter-model interactions to enhance simulation accuracy.
  5. 🚜 Framework or Tool Coupling: Integrating models within a broader DAA framework for a seamless digital representation is a key objective.

Case Study: GRANAR-MECHA

The coupling of GRANAR, a computational tool designed to generate digital versions of monocotyledon root anatomical networks, with MECHA, a model estimating root hydraulic properties, has been transformative. This combination has empowered researchers to:

  1. 🌾 Quantify the effect of individual anatomical features on root’s radial conductivity.
  2. 🌍 Generate virtual anatomies for experimental observations, even those not observed, to estimate corresponding radial conductivities.
  3. 🌦️ Explore the theoretical link between anatomy and radial conductivity, assessing the functional importance of individual anatomical properties.

Case Study: SIMPLACE-CPlantbox

Soil compaction, especially at greater depths, is a growing concern in modern agriculture. To address this, our team have innovatively coupled SIMPLACE with CPlantbox, providing groundbreaking insights into the implications of deep soil compaction and its effects on plant growth. By integrating SIMPLACE, a process-based model focusing on crop-level dynamics, with CRootbox, a detailed root architectural model, our team has:

  1. 🌾 Simulated intricate root-soil interactions, understanding the implications of deep soil compaction.
  2. 🌍 Established a dynamic feedback system ensuring simulated root density is influenced by a myriad of factors, from soil properties to carbon allocation from shoots.
  3. 🌦️ Revealed the intrinsic relationship between soil compaction, weather, and crop species, highlighting the magnified implications of soil compaction in warmer, dryer climates.
  4. 💧 Demonstrated that deep loosening can increase absolute root length density, crucial for sustainable agricultural practices. Explore the detailed investigation
